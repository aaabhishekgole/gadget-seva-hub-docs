🛡️ What is the Cashless Insurance Claim Portal?

The Cashless Insurance Claim Portal is a public-facing web page at /cashless-portal that allows customers or promoters (shopkeepers / sellers) to register an insurance claim, upload required documents, and track the claim status — without needing to log in to the Gadget Seva Hub admin system.

Key Highlights

  • No login or account creation required — open to anyone with an insurance claim number
  • Unique bookmark-able URL per claim (via a secure public token)
  • Guided 4-step progress bar — Register → Upload Docs → Post-Repair → Complete
  • Real-time upload progress indicator for each document
  • Document versioning — re-uploading replaces the previous file cleanly
  • Automatic phase switching: Pre-Repair → Post-Repair after GSH approval
  • All claim actions visible to admin in the Claims Management module
💡 Tip: The portal URL contains a unique token after registration (e.g. /cashless-portal?token=abc-def-123). Bookmark this link to return later and check status or upload more documents.

Local Dev: http://localhost:5173/cashless-portal

📝 Step 1 — Register Your Claim

The first step is to register your insurance claim number in the GSH system. If the same insurance claim number was already registered, the portal returns the existing record — no duplicates are created.

1
Open the portal

Navigate to https://front-end-uat.up.railway.app/cashless-portal in any browser. No login is needed.

2
Enter Insurance Claim Number

Type the claim number issued by your insurance company (e.g. INS-2025-00123). This field is mandatory.

3
Fill in your details

Enter your full name and 10-digit mobile number (mandatory). Email, Promoter Name, Device Model, and IMEI are optional but help GSH process your claim faster.

4
Click "Register Claim →"

The portal creates your claim and immediately moves to the document upload screen. Your browser URL now contains a unique token — bookmark this page.

⚠️ Note: If you register the same insurance claim number again, the portal returns your existing claim — it does not create a duplicate. You can safely re-enter to retrieve your claim link.

📁 Step 2 — Upload Pre-Repair Documents

After registration, you must upload 9 required documents before you can submit the claim for GSH approval. The upload progress bar shows how many you have completed.

1
See the document list

All 9 required slots are shown. Each slot displays an icon, name, and status (Required / Uploaded).

2
Click "Upload" on any slot

A file picker opens. Select a photo (JPG/PNG), PDF, or short video from your device. You can upload in any order.

3
See the slot turn green

Once uploaded, the slot shows a green checkmark and an "Uploaded" label. The progress bar advances.

4
Replace if needed

Click "Replace" on any uploaded slot to upload a newer version. The old file is archived — only the latest version is shown.

💡 After registration, the upload panel appears automatically with all document slots and a progress bar showing 0/9 uploaded.

🚀 Step 3 — Submit for GSH Approval

Once all 9 pre-repair documents are uploaded, the green "Submit for GSH Approval →" button becomes active. Clicking it sends the claim to the GSH team for review.

1
Ensure all 9 documents are uploaded

The progress bar must show 9/9. The Submit button is locked until all documents are present.

2
Click "Submit for GSH Approval →"

The claim status changes to Awaiting GSH Review immediately.

3
Wait for GSH review

The portal shows a message: "Your documents are under review by the Gadget Seva Hub team." You cannot upload new documents during this phase.

⚠️ Note: After submission, the upload panel is hidden until GSH either approves or requests re-uploads. Document uploads are only allowed in CLAIM_SUBMITTED and REUPLOAD_PENDING status.
💡 Once all 9 documents are uploaded, the progress bar shows 9/9 and the green Submit button becomes active.

🔍 Step 4 — GSH Review & Decision

The GSH admin reviews the submitted documents in the Claims Management → Approval Pending tab. They can approve, reject, or request re-upload.

💡 Return to your link to check the outcome. The claim card shows the latest status and a clear message explaining what to do next.

🔧 Step 5 — Upload Post-Repair Documents

After GSH approves the claim, you get your device repaired at the service center. Then return to the portal and upload 7 post-repair documents to prove the repair was completed.

1
Get your device repaired

Take the device to an authorised service center. Keep the repair invoice and excess payment receipt (if applicable).

2
Return to your portal link

The claim card now shows Claim Approved and Step 3 (Post-Repair) is highlighted in the progress bar.

3
Upload all 7 post-repair documents

Photograph the repaired device from 6 angles and upload the service center invoice.

4
Click "Submit Post-Repair Documents →"

All 7 documents must be present. The submit button activates when the progress bar reaches 7/7.

💡 After GSH approves, the portal automatically switches to Step 3 — Post-Repair. The progress bar resets to 0/7 for the new document set.

🔌 Public API Endpoints

All endpoints are under /api/public/cashless/ and require no Authorization header. They are whitelisted in the GSH security configuration.

MethodEndpointPurpose
POST/api/public/cashless/registerRegister new claim or lookup existing by insurance claim number
GET/api/public/cashless/{token}Fetch current claim status, uploaded docs, missing docs
POST/api/public/cashless/{token}/documentsUpload a document (multipart/form-data: documentType + file)
POST/api/public/cashless/{token}/submitSubmit pre-repair docs for GSH approval
POST/api/public/cashless/{token}/post-repairSubmit post-repair docs (only when status is APPROVED)

Sample: Register Claim

POST /api/public/cashless/register
Content-Type: application/json

{
  "insuranceClaimNumber": "INS-2025-00123",
  "customerName":        "Ramesh Kumar",
  "customerPhone":       "9876543210",
  "customerEmail":       "ramesh@example.com",
  "promoterName":        "Galaxy Mobile Shop",
  "deviceModel":         "Samsung Galaxy S24",
  "imeiNumber":          "356938035643809"
}

Content-Type: multipart/form-data

file         = <binary image file>

Full E2E Code Snippet

// TC-E2E-01 — complete lifecycle test
test('complete portal lifecycle', async ({ request }) => {
  const session = await createAdminSession(request);

  // Step 1: Register
  const claim = await registerPublicClaim(request, {
    customerName: 'E2E Test Customer',
    deviceModel:  'Samsung S24 Ultra',
  });
  expect(claim.claimStatus).toBe('CLAIM_SUBMITTED');

  // Step 2: Upload all 9 pre-repair docs
  const afterUpload = await uploadAllPublicPreRepairDocs(request, claim.publicToken);
  expect(afterUpload.canSubmit).toBe(true);

  // Step 3: Submit for approval
  const submitted = await submitPublicClaimForApproval(request, claim.publicToken);
  expect(submitted.claimStatus).toBe('APPROVAL_PENDING');

  // Step 4: Admin approves
  await approvePublicClaimViaAdminApi(request, session.accessToken, claim.claimId);

  // Step 5: Verify POST_REPAIR phase
  const afterApproval = await fetchPublicClaimStatus(request, claim.publicToken);
  expect(afterApproval.portalPhase).toBe('POST_REPAIR');

  // Step 6: Upload 7 post-repair docs
  await uploadAllPublicPostRepairDocs(request, claim.publicToken);

  // Step 7: Submit post-repair → INVOICE_SUBMITTED
  const final = await submitPublicClaimPostRepair(request, claim.publicToken);
  expect(final.claimStatus).toBe('INVOICE_SUBMITTED');
  expect(final.portalPhase).toBe('COMPLETE');
});
