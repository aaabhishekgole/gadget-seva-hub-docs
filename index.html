🚀 Getting Started — Login

Gadget Seva Hub (GSH) is a full-lifecycle device repair management platform. Before you can use any module, you must log in with your credentials.

How to Log In

1
Open your browser

Navigate to the GSH web application URL provided by your administrator (e.g. http://localhost:5173).

2
Enter your credentials

Type your assigned Username and Password in the fields shown below.

3
Click "Sign In"

You will be redirected to the Dashboard. The top-right corner shows your username and role.

🎬 How to Log In

💡 Tip: If you see a "Session expired" message, your login token has timed out. Simply log in again.

📊 Dashboard

The Dashboard gives a real-time snapshot of operations — KPIs, SLA breaches, recent activity, and active escalations.

Dashboard Tabs

Overview SLA / TAT Summary Recent Activities Alerts & Escalations

Overview

Shows total open requests, pickup stats, repair counts, and billing summaries as live KPI cards.

SLA / TAT Summary

Displays turnaround time and breach monitoring for each stage of the repair workflow.

Recent Activities

A live feed of the latest actions across the platform — new requests, pickups, status changes.

Alerts & Escalations

Lists critical issues that need immediate attention — SLA breaches, failed pickups, overdue repairs.

🎬 Dashboard Overview

📋 Service Requests

All device repair jobs start as a Service Request. Every request tracks the device, customer, status, and full history from intake to closure.

Service Request Flow:
Create Request→ Pickup Assigned→ Hub Received→ Service Center→ QC→ Delivery→ Closed

How to Create a Service Request

1
Go to Service Requests → Create Request

Click the "Service Requests" tab in the top navigation, then select "Create Request" from the sub-navigation.

2
Fill in Customer Details

Enter the customer name, mobile number, and address. All fields marked * are mandatory.

3
Fill in Device Details

Select device category, brand, and model. Enter the IMEI number if available.

4
Select Request Type

Choose between Normal Repair or Cashless Claim.

5
Submit

Click "Create Service Request". A unique request number (e.g. GSH-20260412-0001) is generated.

All Requests Queue

Shows every request in the system regardless of status. Use the search and filter options to find specific requests.

🏭 Hub Operations

The Hub is the central warehouse that receives devices from runners, verifies them, and dispatches them to the service center.

Hub Flow:
Device Received→ IMEI Verification→ Inward Register→ Send to Service Center

Device Received at Hub

When a runner drops off a device, the hub operator scans its QR/IMEI to mark it as received.

1
Open Hub Operations → Device Received at Hub
2
Scan or enter the device IMEI or request number
3
Confirm receipt

The device moves to Pending Verification.

Pending Verification

Each received device must be verified — IMEI checked, package condition assessed, photos uploaded.

Send to Service Center

After verification, devices are batched and dispatched to the repair service center.

Inward Register & Inventory

🎬 Hub Operations Workflow

🔧 Service Center

The Service Center handles physical inspection, repair, and total-loss decisions for all devices.

Repair Flow:
Inspection→ Estimate→ Customer Approval→ Repair→ QC

Devices Under Inspection

Newly arrived devices waiting for technician diagnosis.

Estimate Pending & Submitted

After diagnosis the technician creates a cost estimate. "Estimate Pending" = estimate not yet created. "Estimate Submitted" = submitted, awaiting customer decision.

Under Repair & Completed

Total Loss Cases

Devices where repair cost exceeds device value are marked as Total Loss and written off.

🎬 Service Center & Repair Flow

💰 Estimates

Estimates are cost quotations sent to customers for approval. A customer must approve an estimate before repair begins.

Estimate Tabs

New Estimates Awaiting Customer Approval Approved Estimates Rejected Estimates Estimate History

New Estimates

Fresh estimates submitted by the service center, ready for internal review before sending to the customer.

Awaiting Customer Approval

Estimates that have been shared with the customer. The customer approves or rejects via a self-service portal or customer support call.

Approved & Rejected

🛡️ Cashless Approval

Cashless cases are insurance-backed repairs where the customer pays nothing upfront. This module handles the photo evidence and approval workflow before the repair begins.

Approval Queue

Cases with all required photos uploaded, awaiting backend team review and approval.

1
Open Cashless → Approval Queue
2
Review the case details and uploaded photos

Each case requires 6 device photos + 4 damage photos.

3
Click Approve or Reject

Approved cases move to repair. Rejected cases are sent back to the customer for photo re-upload.

Pending Photos

Cases where the customer has not yet uploaded all required evidence photos.

Approved Cases

Cases fully approved and in the repair workflow.

✅ Quality Check

After repair is complete, every device goes through Quality Check (QC) before it is dispatched back to the customer.

QC Flow:
Repair Completed→ QC Inspection→ Pass → Dispatch | Fail → Rework

Pending QC

Devices whose repair is complete and are waiting for a QC technician to inspect them.

QC Results

🚚 Delivery

After QC passes, devices are dispatched back to the customer through the Delivery module.

Delivery Flow:
Ready for Dispatch→ Assign Agent→ Out for Delivery→ Delivered (OTP)

Assign Delivery

Select a delivery agent for devices that are ready to be dispatched.

Delivery Queues

🎬 Quality Check & Delivery

🧾 Billing

The Billing module manages invoice generation, payment collection, reconciliation, and refunds.

Billing Flow:
Generate Invoice→ Send to Customer→ Payment Received→ Reconcile→ Close

Generate Invoice

Once a repaired device is delivered, a GST-compliant invoice is created for the customer.

1
Open Billing → Generate Invoice
2
Search for the service request
3
Review line items and totals

Parts, labour, GST are auto-calculated.

4
Click Generate

Invoice PDF is created and can be sent to the customer.

Payment Reconciliation

Records UTR (payment reference) numbers against invoices to confirm payments received from customers.

Refund Cases

Manages partial or full refunds for cases where repair was unsuccessful or customer is due a credit.

📑 Claims Management (Cashless Claims)

The Claims module manages end-to-end cashless insurance claims — from initial registration through approval, invoice submission, and insurance payout.

Full Claim Lifecycle:
Claim Registered→ Docs Verified→ Approved→ Repair + Invoice→ Insurance→ Closed
⚠️ Role required: Claims are accessible to Admin, Backend Team, Customer Support, and MSE Team.

All Claims

Master list of every cashless claim in the system. Use the tabs or filter dropdown to narrow by status.

Claims Sub-Tabs

All Claims Approval Pending Approved Claims Rejected Claims Re-upload Pending Claim History

Approval Pending

Claims submitted by customers awaiting backend team document verification and approval.

1
Click the "Approval Pending" tab

Lists all claims in APPROVAL_PENDING status.

2
Open a claim by clicking "View Details"

Review the submitted documents, IMEI, and claim details.

3
Approve or Reject

Click Approve (claim moves to APPROVED) or Reject (enter reason, claim moves to REJECTED).

Approved & Rejected

Re-upload Pending

Claims where the customer needs to re-submit photos or documents. The system tracks the number of re-upload attempts.

🎬 Cashless Claim Lifecycle

🧮 Invoice Verification

After a cashless repair is complete, the service center submits an invoice. This module is used to verify and approve or reject that invoice before it is sent to the insurance company.

Invoice Verification Flow:
Invoice Submitted→ Backend Reviews→ Approve / Reject→ Ready for Insurance

Invoice Queue

All invoices submitted by the service center and waiting for backend verification.

1
Open Invoice Verification → Invoice Queue
2
Click on a claim to view the invoice

Review the invoice amount, line items, and uploaded invoice document.

3
Approve the invoice

Enter the approved amount. If the amount exceeds the threshold, admin approval is required.

4
Or reject with reason

The service center will need to re-upload a corrected invoice.

🏦 Insurance Submission

The final step in the cashless claim lifecycle. Once an invoice is verified and approved, the claim package is submitted to the insurance company for reimbursement.

Insurance Submission Flow:
Invoice Approved→ Package Ready→ Submit to Insurer→ Claim Closed
⚠️ Role required: Insurance Submission is accessible to Admin, Backend Team, and MSE Team only.

Ready for Insurance

Claims whose invoices have been approved and are ready to be packaged and submitted to the insurance company.

1
Open Insurance Submission → Ready for Insurance
2
Review the claim summary

Check the approved amount, documents, and claim number.

3
Click "Submit to Insurance"

Add submission notes and confirm. The claim status changes to SUBMITTED_TO_INSURANCE.

🔔 Notifications

Tracks all SMS and email messages sent to customers and staff. Use this module to diagnose delivery failures and monitor communication logs.

When to Use

  • Customer says they didn't receive an OTP or notification — check SMS Logs for their number.
  • Investigate delivery failures in the Failed Notifications tab.
  • Audit all communications for a specific service request.

📈 Reports

Generate operational and financial reports for management review and client communication.

Available Reports

  • Request Report — new requests, open vs closed, by date range
  • Pickup Report — runner productivity, on-time rates
  • Repair Report — repair throughput and outcomes
  • Delivery Report — dispatch and completion analytics
  • SLA / TAT Report — breach analysis and turnaround trends
  • Revenue Report — billing totals, reconciled vs outstanding

⚙️ Settings

System-wide configuration. Only Admins can access Settings.

What You Can Configure

  • Status Configuration — define and manage workflow states
  • SLA Configuration — set turnaround time targets per request type
  • Notification Settings — manage SMS/email retry policies and channels
  • File Storage Config — configure storage bucket and signed URL settings
  • System Preferences — global platform switches

🔍 Audit & Logs

Enterprise-grade audit trail. Every significant action in the platform is recorded with a timestamp, user, and before/after snapshot.

Use Cases

🏃 Runner Flow — Field Pickup Agent

Pickup runners (field agents) have their own separate web portal and mobile app. They do not log into the main admin portal. Instead, they use the Runner App or receive an SMS deep-link to their pickup job.

Runner Flow:
Admin Assigns Pickup→ Runner Gets SMS→ Opens Runner App / Link→ Confirms Pickup + Photos→ Drops at Hub

Step 1 — Runner App Login

Runners log into the Runner App at /runner-app using their mobile number and password set during onboarding.

1
Open the Runner App URL

Admin shares /runner-app with the runner. The Android APK handles this automatically on mobile.

2
Sign in with mobile number or username

Default password is Runner@ + last 4 digits of mobile number (set during onboarding).

Step 2 — Runner Inbox

After login the runner sees all pickup assignments with customer name, device, scheduled slot, and an "Open Pickup Flow" button per job.

3
Click "Open Pickup Flow"

Opens the Runner Portal — shows customer address, IMEI confirmation, and photo upload.

4
Upload device photos

Capture required photos (front, back, sides, damage) and upload through the portal.

5
Confirm pickup

Click "Confirm Pickup". The request moves to "Picked Up" in the admin portal automatically.

6
Drop device at hub

Physically deliver to the hub. Hub operator scans it to complete the handoff.

Step 3 — SMS Deep Link (One-Tap Alternative)

When a pickup is assigned, the runner gets an SMS with a one-click link. Tapping it opens the runner portal directly — no login needed.

📱 Android APK: If the runner has the GSH Android app installed, the SMS link opens it directly with the pickup pre-loaded. Otherwise it falls back to the browser portal.
